Per-track stint-length guidance (official Pirelli race previews)

Honest result of this research: we initially wired these sourced numbers in directly as each compound's maximum stint length, and it made the model's own recommendations measurably worse (stop-count agreement against real 2026 races dropped from 78/134 to 65/134). The reason: a Pirelli preview's "can cover 35 laps without catastrophic degradation" describes what a tyre can survive, not what a team should choose to run, real strategists usually pit well before that point for reasons (track position, undercut risk) our degradation model doesn't fully price in. Loosening our ceiling to match let the optimiser exploit stints that are technically legal but not realistic. These figures are now wired in as a tightening-only check (they only shorten our estimate, never lengthen it), and for every track below that currently has no effect, since our existing estimates were already more conservative than Pirelli's stated survivable maximums. Kept here as a validated cross-check and for any future track where that might not hold.
